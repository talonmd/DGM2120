Egg Terminology

Here are some defintions to terms you may come across in your research:

yolk
You may know that this is the yellow ball in the middle of the egg, but did you know that the egg yolk is there to provide nutrients to the developing embryo of the growing chick? Not only that, but over half of the nutrition in eggs is stored in the yolk, whether fertilized or unfertilized.
Fun Facts:
The color of the yolk is dependent upon the diet of the hen who laid the egg.
Hens can sometimes lay an egg with two yolks. This is commom among young hens whose reproductive cycles have not yet been solidified.
albumen
It is knows to us by its more popular name, egg whites. The albumen makes up over half of the eggs weight. It serves as a protection for the egg yolk, and stores over half of the eggs protien.
Fun Fact: The older the egg is, the more translucent the albumen is.
bloom
The bloom of an egg is a thin, protective layer that is outside the shell. It stops bacteria and dust from entering in, and is vital for the survival of fertilized eggs. When the egg is washed, rubbed or gets wet, the bloom is washed or wiped away.
